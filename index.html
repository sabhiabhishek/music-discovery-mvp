🎧 Discovery Companion

Not another playlist — a listening partner you can talk to. Tell it what you already love and what you want more or less of, and it negotiates with you in real time, explaining why every pick fits. This is a live prototype: every reply below is generated live by Claude, not scripted.

Set your taste above, pick (or type) what you want to explore, and hit send — I'll suggest 3 tracks and explain the thread connecting them to what you already love. You can then push back — "too mellow," "more upbeat," "closer to my taste" — and I'll adjust live, in the same conversation.
Why this needs AI, not a better recommendation algorithm: Collaborative filtering and embedding similarity can tell you what's statistically close to what you already play — that's exploitation, not exploration, and it's exactly the loop users are complaining about. It can't hold a conversation, take an explicit instruction like "weirder, but not louder," reason across genres that share no listener overlap (e.g. why 1970s Ethiopian jazz connects to a Khruangbin fan), or explain itself in language a human trusts. An LLM can do all four in one turn, and adjust again next turn — turning discovery from a passive weekly drop into an active, steerable dialogue the user controls.
Prototype scope: a 40-track illustrative reference catalog stands in for Spotify's full library so the reasoning pattern can be evaluated end-to-end. In production, track selection would call Spotify's catalog/audio-features API; Claude's role — intent parsing, cross-genre reasoning, and conversational negotiation — stays the same.
